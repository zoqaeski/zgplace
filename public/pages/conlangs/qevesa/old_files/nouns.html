Qevesa Grammar

Nominal Morphology

Qevesa makes a distinction between proper and common nouns. All proper nouns are irregular in the written form in that grammatical suffixes are separated with colons. Despite this, the pronunciation is as if the noun were a common noun.

Number

Qevesa possesses three numbers, singular, plural and quantitative.

Postconsonantal Postvocalic
Singular sg -Ø Unmarked
Plural pl -v -(e)v Generic; used when a more numerals or quantifiers are not marked.
Quantitative qt -c -(e)c Used with numerals and quantifiers.

Possession

Possession is normally marked by a proclitic particle, ké, unless the possessor is a pronoun, in which case the pronoun is declined to the genetive case.

Pronouns

Pronouns are roughly equivalent to nouns in terms of syntax and morphology, although they tend to have some irregular declensions.

Personal Pronouns

Most pronouns refer only to animate referents; a separate inanimate pronoun is used for inanimate referents. Pronouns are entirely without gender, and make no disctinction between male/female/neuter. There are two first person plural pronouns, an inclusive, which includes the listener, and an exclusive, which does not.

Quantifiers

Quantifiers are similar to determiners. They do not agree in case with the noun they modify; as a rule, quantifiers are always marked in the genetive case.

Type Quantifier Prefix
Paucal laifik laif- a few of ...
Plural véjik véj- many of ...
Partitive nílak níl- some ...
Selective përek për- some ... / each ...
Exhaustive véqek véq- all of ...

Articles

Qevesa has only the indefinite article, however it is used fairly infrequently.

The Indefinite Article

The indefinite article, sel, is a variant of the word for the numeral “one”, and as such is only used when referring to a single example of the head noun. A consequence of this is that the indefinite article is used with the quantitative plural form of the noun.
