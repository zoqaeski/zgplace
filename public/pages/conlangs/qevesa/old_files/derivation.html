Qevesa Grammar

Roots of Words and Derivational Morphology

Qevesa is a language whose roots are primarily triconsonantal or biconsonantal. Various forms for verbs, nouns and other derived forms are created by placing vowels between, before, or after the consonants, or by adding affixes (usually suffixes). For ease of discussion, in this grammar, the positions within the root are named as follows: C1, C2, C3 are the three consonants, and the positions adjacent to them are P0, P12, P23, P4. The citation form of the root consists of C1-C2-C3; e.g. ‘write’ is s-k-p.

Nominalisations

Due to the triconsonantal nature of Qevesa roots, most nouns are formed by changing vowels within a verb form. Many noun forms end in -a, as this is the dechticaetiative singular indefinite case ending. For more information on the plural forms, see the nouns page.

Process

CuCáCa

This is a general nominalisation naming a process, activity or ability.

  • sukápa writing
  • qutáva sight
  • nutála thinking
  • dumána creation
  • lukáša listening, hearing
  • sunána speaking

Instance

CaCuCa

A single instance of the action.

  • sakupa a writing session
  • qatuva a glance
  • natula a thought
  • damuna the formation of a single object
  • lakuša an act of listening
  • sanuna an utterance

Resulting Object

CiCCa

An object resulting from the action.

  • sikpa a document, an essay
  • qitva a vision
  • nitla an idea
  • dimna a pot, a vessel
  • sinna a speech

Actor

CoCiCa

A person that does the action.

  • sokipa a writer
  • qotiva a watcher, an observer
  • notila a thinker, a philosopher
  • domina a craftsman
  • lokiša a listener
  • sonina a speaker, an orator

Patient

CiCéCa

A person or object that undergoes the action.

  • siképa a text
  • diména the form or shape of something
  • likéša a sound
  • sinéna an audience

Device

CeCóCa

An object that performs the action.

  • sekópa a writing device
  • qetóva camera
  • netóla a computer
  • demóna mold
  • lekóša a recording device
  • senóna a speaking device

Instrument

CéCiCa

An object used to perform the action.

  • sékipa a pen
  • qétiva an eye
  • nétila a mind
  • démina a potter's wheel
  • lékiša an ear
  • sénina a mouth

Location

CéCóCa

A place where the action occurs.

  • sékópa a desk
  • qétóva a lookout, an observatory
  • nétóla a study
  • démóna a pottery shop
  • lékóša an auditorium
  • sénóna a podium

Representation

CiCoCa

A representation of the action

  • sikopa a book
  • kitosa a fish
  • piloma (a) blue (thing)

Suffixes

The following suffixes can be added to some nouns:

  • -{CM}kait collection of; e.g. sikopa "book" → sikophkaita "library"
  • -lain inhabitant of; e.g. Qevelia "Qevelia" → qevelilain "person from Qevelia"
