Person

Aspects and Moods

Perfective

The perfective aspect indicates an action viewed as a single whole.

Imperfective

The imperfective aspect indicates continuous or habitual states and actions.

Retrospective

The retrospective aspect refers to a previous action viewed from the perspective of a later time.

Indicative

The indicative is used for factual statements and positive beliefs.

Conditional

The conditional refers to an action whose realisation is dependent on a certain condition.

Potential

The potential is a mood of probability, indicating that the action or occurrence is considered likely.

Cohortative

The cohortative is used to express plea, insistence, imploring, self-encouragement, wish, desire, intent, command, purpose or consequence.

Admirative

The admirative is used to express surprise, doubt and occasionally sarcasm.

Imperative

The imperative is used to indicate commands. It is only used with the second person, and in contrast to the others, consists of the infinitive with the person suffix.

English examples of the usage of the above aspects and moods: (using "to speak")

  Indicative Conditional Potential Cohortative Admirative Imperative
Perfective I speak. I would speak. I can speak. I should speak. I speak?
Imperfective I am speaking. I would be speaking. I can be speaking. I should be speaking. I am speaking?
Retrospective I've spoken. I would've spoken. I could've spoken. I should've spoken. I 've spoken?

Negation

Negation in Qevesa is achieved through use of the adverbial particle niširo.

A Special Case: The Copula

As in most other languages, the copula is an irregular verb. It can exist as a stative suffix on an adjective or adverb for simple subject-predicate clauses, or as a stand alone verb in more complicated clauses. It normally uses the imperfective aspect to represent continuous states and the perfective aspect to represent temporary states; a completed state will use the retrospective aspect.

Lisa turlotuottutt, ëki zibisa ottukkar.
Lisa.DCHT this.one.COP.PFV, 1SG.GEN sister-DCHT COP-IPV-FOC
This is my sister Lisa. (This one is Lisa, my sister she is being)

Person is not marked on the copula except:

  • when the subject is referenced with a focus pronoun;
  • when confusion or ambiguity would result from not doing so;
  • or for special emphasis.
