Qevesa Grammar

Verbal Morphology

Verb Roots and Classes

Qevesa has six classes of verbs, which differ in the final character of the root. These can be readily distinguished by the process used to form the infinitive.

  • Class 1 verbs have roots ending in a consonant, -i, -u or -y.
  • Class 2 verbs have roots ending in -a or -ä.
  • Class 3 verbs have roots ending in -e or -ö.
  • Class 4 verbs have roots ending in -ë.
  • Class 5 verbs have roots ending in -o.
  • Class 6 verbs comprise the majority of irregular verbs, and end in -uor(-) with an optional final vowel.

There are also a number of irregular verbs, which will be discussed later.

The Infinitive

The infinitive is formed by the following processes; stem final vowels are dropped where they exist:

Verb Class Suffix
1 -ul
2 -aul
3 -öl
4 -jol
5 -uol
6 -ajal

Person

Verbs are often conjugated to person to indicate the agent. Personal forms are created by adding the personal pronoun suffix to the verb stem; the following table outlines the endings.

1 sg -ër
2 sg -tar
3 sg -mir
1 pl -jor
2 pl -tur
3 pl -mur
Focus -kar

When the agent does not consist of a pronoun, conjugation to person is optional. In this case, the final character of the verb form is duplicated.

Aspect and Mood

Qevesa has five aspects (stative imperfective, dynamic imperfective, perfective, retrospective, delimitative), five moods (indicative, conditional, potential, cohortative, imperative). Tense is not indicated on the verb; if time must be indicated it is done by using a noun in the essive case. A verb cannot have more than one aspect, nor can it possess more than a single mood. These moods/aspects are formed using the following patterns:

Aspect/Mood Formation
Stative Imperfective -v(-)
Dynamic Imperfective -k(-)
Perfective -t(-)
Retrospective -s(-)
Delimitative -q(-)
Indicative -Ø(-)
Conditional -om(-)
Potential -es(-)
Cohortative -an(-)
Imperative Consists of the infinitive + -ir.

When the person is not explicitly marked, the final letter of the aspect/mood suffix is doubled on all but the imperative.

Particles

There are a number of particles, normally enclitic, that may be used to indicate additional information. When there is more than one particle attached to a verb, they are fusionally conjoined.

Active Voice

Intrasitive verbs may be followed by the particle le, which basically states that the sole argument was in control of the action rather than being simply an experiencer.

Telicity

Telicity, or resulativity, can be indicated with the particle kuo.

Negation

Negation in Qevesa is achieved through use of the adverbial/prepositional particle niširo ("not"). Alternatively, negation may be indicated by the postpositonal particle në.
