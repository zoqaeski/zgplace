Qevesa Grammar

Nominal Syntax

Use of the Cases

Dechticaetiative Case

The dechticaetiative case marks the experiencer of intransitive sentences, as well as adjectives directly modifying the experiencer.

As oto ëzykutt.
DEF bird.DCHT fly-PFV
The bird flies.

The dechticaetiative is also used to mark the theme of ditransitive sentences.

Kiavan etoi ňokoita Žulijas turisti kevtast voitutt.
Peter two.DCHT book.NPL-DCHT Julia-ACC this.ESS morning.ESS take.PFV
Peter took Julia two books this morning.

Ergative case:

The ergative case is used to mark the agent and/or donor of transitive and ditransitive sentences.

Johan lupos tetött.
John.ERG apple.ACC eat.PFV
John ate an apple.
Kiavan etoi ňokoita Žulijas turisti kevtast voitutt.
Peter two.DCHT book.NPL-DCHT Julia-ACC this.ESS morning.ESS take.PFV
Peter took Julia two books this morning.

Personal pronouns in the ergative case are usually omitted except for emphasis, as the personal endings on the verb convey the agent.

Accusative case:

The accusative case is used to mark the patient of transitive sentence.

Johan lupos tetött.
John.ERG apple.ACC eat.PFV
John ate an apple.

It is also used to mark the recipient of ditransitive sentences.

Kiavan etoi ňokoita Žulijas turisti kevtast voitutt.
Peter two.DCHT book.NPL-DCHT Julia-ACC this.ESS morning.ESS take.PFV
Peter took Julia two books this morning.

Partitive case:

This is a seldom used case that was used in a similar manner to the dechticaetiative and accusative cases, when there is no specific result or amount. It is mostly now used as a plural marker, after which the desire case suffix is attached.

Genitive case

The genetive case marks the possessor of another noun or quantity, and turns the noun into a pseudo-adjective, which does not agree in case with its head.

Kiavaki ňokoit
Peter.GEN book.DPL
Peter's books

The genetive may also be used to mark association, especially with the postposition om(y) ("about", "regarding").

Essive case

duration, or to indicate time;

indicates a temporary state of existence.

Translative case

indicates a more-or-less permanent change of state

Postpositional Case

to mark additional information that requires a postposition, such as location, instrument, etc

Articles

The Definite Article

The defintite article is used more frequently in Qevesa than in English. It is used:

  • Before nouns used in an abstract or a general sense:
    e.g. As oromavas eloikautër – I like horses (lit. I like the horses).
  • Before each noun in a collection or series of nouns.
  • Before expressions of measure and weight.

The Indefinite Article

Strictly speaking, there is no indefinite article in Qevesa; the numeral one is used instead. Consequently, it is not used as often as its English counterpart, as it specifically expresses the concept of "one". One unusual use of the indefinite article is to negate nouns. Unlike the definite article, the indefinite article agrees in case with the noun it modifies.
