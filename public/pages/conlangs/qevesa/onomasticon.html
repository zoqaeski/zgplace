Introduction

An interesting effect of the nature of Qevesa verbal constructions is that many names are formed of single-word sentences, often expressing a prediction of the future, a reflection on the parents’ state-of-mind, or similar. The other main derivation for names is from compound adjectives expressing qualities favoured of the child, usually relating to strength for males, and beauty for females.

Generally, male names end in Ca, Ci, Co, Cjo or Cu, and female names end in Cja, Ce, Cë, Ci or Cy, although there are many exceptions to the rule.
