Qevelian Railway Signalling

Route and Speed indicators

Route Indicators

Main signals only provide track occupancy and speed indications. This is quite adequate for most locations, where there are few diverging routes; however, there are a number of cases where provision of route indicators may be necessary, such as when a medium speed indication may take the train towards a number of different routes. There are a number of different designs of route indicators; these are described below, each in a separate section. As Qevelian signalling primarily indicates speed over route, the route indicators are provided only when necessary.

Direction Indicators

These are the most common form of route indicator. They consist of row of two to six white lights which are lit to indicate the geographical direction; a single light indicates the leftmost route, and subsequent lights indicate which route from the leftmost.

Speed Indications

Medium Speed Indicators

Main signals can only indicate a single reduced speed for diverging routes at turnouts, referred to as Medium Speed. To provide a higher speed limit where 60 km/h or faster turnouts were installed, an additional pair of aspects was introduced by wiring the signals to pulsate or flash their lights. A sign displaying the actual turnout speed was also introduced, and this has since been supplemented by illuminated indicators capable of displaying multiple speed restrictions.

Originally medium speed indicators only applied to diverging routes within the station limits, up until the train has passed a Resume Line Speed sign. However, with the introduction of solid-state computerised interlocking, the illuminated indicators have been used to implement “train speed optimisation”. This is a technique that attempts to increase train headways by reducing the need for trains to brake and accelerate as they approach an occupied block section. At present, it is in use only on heavily-trafficked sections, such as some lines in the Kirua metropolitan area.

Numerical speed indicators for main signals are always located above the signal head, and those for distant signals below the signal head. At combined main/distant signals, up to two illuminated indicators may be provided if necessary.

Commence Medium Speed signs

These signs are provided at turnouts where a medium speed restriction applies. Normally the speed restriction applies from the signal, but in some locations, such as those with multiple turnout speeds, a sign will indicate where the speed restriction begins.

Resume Line Speed signs

These signs are provided at the end of station limits to indicate that any speed restrictions that were displayed by Medium Speed signs are no longer valid.

Permanent Speed limit signs

There are a variety of signs in use that indicate the speed limit for a given section of track. These signs are located adjacent to the track. Note that the actual maximum speed of a particular train is listed in the working timetable; this may be lower than the signposted limit, but will never be higher.

Temporary Speed Restrictions

Temporary speed restrictions are indicated by fixed signs located 50 metres before the affected portion of track. Unlike permanent speed restrictions, temporary speed restrictions always have an advance warning sign, located up to 1200 metres before the affected portion. The end of temporary speed restriction sign is located 50 metres after the affected portion of track.
