Qevelian Railway Signalling

Signals on Vehicles

There are a number of different signals and signs displayed on moving and stationary vehicles.

Signals on Train Movements

Headlights

The leading end of a train movement is indicated by three white lights arranged in a triangle with the peak facing up. Headlights must be displayed by both day and night, but must be dimmed when:

  • approaching locations where train is scheduled to stop;
  • standing within station limits;
  • approaching trains in the opposite direction, until the locomotives or leading cabs have passed each other. The headlight must then be returned to ‘full on’.

Tail lights

The rear end of a train movement is indicated by one or two flashing red lights, and an end-of-train marker. Steady lights are permitted if lights are unable to be flashed.

Other lights

The rear end of a locomotive is indicated by a single white light located on the lower left-hand side. Multiple-unit consists operating as a single unit need only display tail light on rear locomotive.

Assisting locomotives with a separate driver display both headlights and a rear light if leading. Non-leading locomotives propelled by an assisting locomotive display only the rear light.

Timetabled light-engine movements must display headlights and tail lights. Multiple locomotives operating as a light movement need only display headlights on first locomotive and tail lights on last locomotive; intermediate locomotives need not display white rear lights.

Shunting Movements

Shunting movements are indicated by two horizontal white lights on the leading end. Light shunting locomotives must display two horizontal white lights on each end.
