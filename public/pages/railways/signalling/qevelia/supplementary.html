Qevelian Railway Signalling

Supplementary Signals

There are a number of supplementary signals that indicate additional information not conveyed by main or shunting signals. They may be valid for one or both types of train movements.

Brake Test and Departure signals

These signals are found in any location where trains may be remarshalled. They instruct the driver to perform a brake test so that shunters can confirm the integrity of the train. They are often combined with departure signals, which are used by the signaller and/or guard to give the proceed order.

Variations of Brake Test/Departure signals. An obsolete design had all four lights vertically inline, with the green at the bottom.

Aspect Indication
Ignore
Ignore this signal.
Apply Brake
The brake test has commenced; driver is instructed to apply train brakes.
Release Brakes
The driver is instructed to release the train brakes.
Brake Test Complete
The brake test has completed successfully.
Depart Order
The driver is permitted to depart.

A main signal that permits a departure when cleared will have a Depart Order plate below the main signal head.

Note that the dwarf signal at the base of the main signal displays Route Clear when the signal is cleared, regardless of the aspect of the main signal.

Proceed Indicators

Proceed indicators are a type of repeater signal that show whether the associated main signal has been cleared. They are used in locations where the starting signal cannot be clearly seen from the normal halting place of trains, and as a type of group starting signal where a single main signal applies to a number of tracks.

Proceed indicators also double as Depart Order signals.

Aspect Indication
The main signal is not cleared.
The main signal has been cleared; proceed at a maximum of 36 km/h until main signal is clearly visible.
Note that the speed restriction only applies if the train was stopped at the station; trains that do not stop can safely ignore this signal.

Proceed indicators may be combined with Brake Test/Departure signals, theatre-type multi-purpose indicators, and repeater/distant signals. Note that the repeating light is not displayed when a proceed indicator is combined with a distant signal.
