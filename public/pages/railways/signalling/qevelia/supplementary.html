Qevelian Railway Signalling

Supplementary Signals

There are a number of supplementary signals that indicate additional information not conveyed by main or shunting signals. They may be valid for one or both types of train movements.

Protection Signals

Protection signals are used to control train and shunting movements. They are used to divide a station into sections, or as group exit signals.

Protection signals may occur either alone or integrated into the backboard for a main signal. When integrated with a main signal, they nearly always include shunting indicators and caution signals.

Variations of protection and main signal combinations. The three white lights are lit in various combinations to indicate various additional aspects.

Protection signal aspects

Stop

Train must stop before the signal.

Route Clear

Route is locked and free of obstructions up to the next main signal.

Caution Signals

Caution signals are used to permit a train to pass a signal at Stop. The driver must be prepared to stop before any obstacle. This aspect is often used to allow two train movements to couple.

Short Entry Route Indicators

These signals are used to announce a short entry route at Accepting signals, such as that into a dead-end road or short siding. At loops designed for simultaneous crossings (i.e., non-stop crossing movements), this signal announces that such a move will take place.

This indicator can only be displayed in conjunction with a signal displaying Medium or Route Clear.

Note that the distant aspect of the combined signal is masked. Medium speed indicators, if present on the main signal, will also be switched off.

Brake Test and Departure signals

These signals are found in any location where trains may be remarshalled. They instruct the driver to perform a brake test so that shunters can confirm the integrity of the train. They are often combined with departure signals, which are used by the signaller and/or guard to give the proceed order.

Variations of Brake Test/Departure signals. An obsolete design had all four lights vertically inline, with the green at the bottom.

Aspect Indication
Ignore
Ignore this signal.
Apply Brake
The brake test has commenced; driver is instructed to apply train brakes.
Release Brakes
The driver is instructed to release the train brakes.
Brake Test Complete
The brake test has completed successfully.
Depart Order
The driver is permitted to depart.

A main signal that permits a departure when cleared will have a Depart Order plate below the main signal head.

Proceed Indicators

Proceed indicators are a type of repeater signal that show whether the associated main signal has been cleared. They are used in locations where the Starting signal cannot be clearly seen from the normal halting place of trains, and as a type of group Starting signal where a single main signal applies to a number of tracks.

Proceed indicators also double as Depart Order signals.

Aspect Indication
The main signal is not cleared.
The main signal has been cleared; proceed at a maximum of 36 km/h until main signal is clearly visible.
Note that the speed restriction only applies if the train was stopped at the station; trains that do not stop can safely ignore this signal.

Proceed indicators may be combined with Brake Test/Departure signals, theatre-type multi-purpose indicators, and repeating signals, although the latter is uncommon.
