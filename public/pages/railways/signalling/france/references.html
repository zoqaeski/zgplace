5.1 About this Document

This is a chapter on French railway signalling, consisting primarily of the SNCF with growing material on the CP and RENFE. Please notice that this document is copyrighted, with any reuse requiring permission.

Any comments, corrections, or suggestions would be greatly appreciated. Note that while that this paper assumes some familiarity with signaling, questions and comments to improve the article for those not familiar with signaling will be considered.

This version is 2.1.2, HTML conversion with minor revisions of text version 1.1.1 of 1994, which is a minor revision of version 1.1.0 of 1991.

Priority items for future revisions consist of:

  • new signalling developments since 1991
  • substantial information on CP and RENFE signalling
  • track diagrams with mechanical signals
  • ex-AL Germanic mechanical signals on the SNCF
  • non-TGV cab signals
  • further information on early light signals in France
  • dimensions of signal heads and lenses
Any additional sources of information in these areas would be greatly appreciated, and acknowledged.

Specific questions and missing information

  • What does SAS stand for, and why is not (S) used instead?
  • What are all valid indications for TECS/TSCS in addition to VL [e.g. (VL), (A), A, S]?
  • Is TECS/TSCS used with RR, i.e. for 30 km/h crossovers?
  • Are projected destination indicators (G, D, SAS, Imp) white or yellow?
  • What are all valid indications with TIP in addition to VL and M?
  • What are the exact head layouts of the historical colorlight signals?
    • relative placement of colors in 1885-1941 transitional heads
    • exact layout of colors in Etat St. Mariens head
    • layout of early 1941 code rectangular heads
  • What is the configuration of the Nord Nf signal head located on overhead gantries? The C aspect is the only one confirmed.
  • Do the 1885 colorlight heads have a white light for the clear clear indication, or is dark considered clear? It appears from photos that a white was added later abovethe main head.
