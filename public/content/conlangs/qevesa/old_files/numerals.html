Qevesa Grammar

Numerals

Qevesa uses a bijective decimal system, as is the standard virtually Teranë-wide. A bijective numeral system is one that does not use a digit to represent zero; rather, it has a digit to represent its base. The numerals used in Teranë are:

ɛ 1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8 9 ȣ

Multi-digit numbers in written form are hyphenated at every odd digit greater than 3 (hundreds), and individual number lexemes are conjoined with -tav- (and) between lexemes and -et before hyphens; e.g. 3A212 (56610) is kwlišovistët-ketkištatavëti, 914812 (1575210) is ostokwiëtstavidekwlkitavšovistët-qyšštatavyksëtti. Fractions smaller than sezënëtstavi (1/1210) are written with sezën- prefixing the greatest numeral.

Unlike ordinary adjectives, numerals do not decline to case.

Measurements

As could be expected of a culture that counts using a duodecimal system, the measurement system is also based on units of twelve.

Length

The following units are the standard for length:
