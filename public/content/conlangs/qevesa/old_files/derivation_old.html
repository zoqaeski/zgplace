Qevesa Grammar

Derivational Morphology

There are eight parts of speech:

  1. nouns;
  2. pronouns;
  3. adjectives & adverbs;
  4. verbs;
  5. adpositions;
  6. conjunctions;
  7. particles;
  8. and interjections.

Adpositions are like prepositions in English, except they usually follow their objects (but may occasionally precede them). They could be considered merely as particles, but there are a number of particles in Qevesa that do not act as adpositions. Only nouns, pronouns, adjectives and verbs can be inflected, however by use of certain suffixes and infixes nearly any type of word can be made into another.

The dictionary or stem form of a noun is just that — it cannot be used except as a lexeme onto which affixes are attached. A noun in the dechticaetiative case can form a complete sentence, however.

To create an adjective from a noun or verb, add -i; an adverb has -iro.

To create a noun from a noun, adjective or verb, the following suffixes can be added:

  • -dok place where the action occurs
  • -kait collection of; e.g. ňoko "book" → ňokokait "library"
  • -ja(ul) action relating to stem; e.g. ňoko "book" → ňokojaul "(to) write"
  • -lain inhabitant of; e.g. Qevelia "Qevelia" → qevelilain "person from Qevelia"
  • -min tool; e.g. ňokojaut "(to) write → ňokojamin "letter (of the alphabet)"
  • -olys study of
  • -ukš agent, one who does
