Automatic Block Sequences

There are several different sequences used, differing in the number of clear blocks before an occupied block.

Two-Aspect

Two-aspect block signals are rarely used when distant signals are not provided, as the sighting distance is less than the braking distance. The earliest automatic block systems used two-aspect motorised semaphores with motorised distant signals at braking distance in rear.

Motorised semaphores remain in use on short sections of just two lines at present.

Three-Aspect

Three-aspect automatic block is fairly common, especially on older lines. As it is relatively easily upgraded to a four-aspect system, most installations have been modified to permit higher speeds.

Four-Aspect

The most common automatic block system, as it permits a mixture of fast and slow trains. Faster and heavier trains start braking at the Advance Caution, other trains brake at the Caution signal.

An older style of four-aspect block used Approach Medium instead of Advance Caution, but fell out of favour with the introduction of flashing aspects.
