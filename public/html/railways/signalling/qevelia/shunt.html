Qevelian Railway Signalling

Signals for Shunting

Shunting signals are specialised designs normally found only in stations and yards. Most are only applicable to shunting movements, as these are distinguished from train movements. None of the designs are capable of displaying speed indications, and in most cases do not display route occupancy either.

Shunting signals

These signals are used to control movements between main lines and sidings, and occasionally between sidings. Their indications are only applicable for shunting movements; train movements are to ignore them.

There are a number of different designs of shunting signal, but all display the same indications.

Where mechanical signalling has been replaced with colour lights, shunting signals have often been replaced with a colour light variant. Unlike mechanical signals, colour-light shunting signals may not be located on the same post as a main signal to indicate a shunting route; they will be replaced with smaller shunting indicators below the main signal head(s) that will operate in conjuction with the main signal.

Shunting Signal Aspects

Stop for Shunting Movements

Shunting movements must stop short of the signal.

Shunting Movements may Proceed

Shunting movements may commence or continue shunting.

Shunt permission indicators

If indication that shunting is permitted is required at a main signal, a shunt permission light may be embedded into the main head. These signals also double as calling-on signals, by pulsating the white light.

Aspects

Stop

Both train and shunting movements must stop before the signal.

Shunting Permitted

Train movements must stop before the signal.
Shunting movements may start or continue, although the route may be blocked.

Calling-On

Train movements must stop before the signal, and then proceed at no greater than 24 km/h, prepared to stop before any obstacles. Route may be blocked.
Shunting movements may start or continue, although the route may be blocked.

Route Clear

Both train and shunting movements may proceed; route is free of obstructions.
