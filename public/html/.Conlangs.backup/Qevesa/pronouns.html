Qevesa Grammar

Pronouns

Personal Pronouns

Qevesa is a pro-drop language, as subject pronouns of the first and second person are usually omitted before the verb; by not doing so, the speaker gives the subject special emphasis. The same may also be done for third person, but this occurs much less frequently.

Reflexive and Reciprocal pronouns

There is only a single reflexive pronoun, vyň, used for reflexive constructions within a single clause:

  • Kepas velaukër, vëksis vyňa kaštautër. "I want to buy myself a car."

Reciprocal constructions also use vyň; the reciprocal pronoun riauty is only used where misunderstandings may occur. Both of these pronouns decline to the seven cases (but not to number):

Possessive Pronouns

For the most part, possession is indicated by use of the personal pronoun in the genetive case. However, if the possession is reflexive, the reflexive pronoun (in the genetive case) is used instead.

Demonstrative Pronouns

There are three degrees of distance expressed with demonstrative pronouns in Qevesa, near to the speaker, near to the addressee, and distant from both. Demonstrative pronouns are declined to all six cases and three numbers, using a similar construct to nouns, with the exception that they all end in -e. Relative Pronouns

Relative pronouns are used as referential markers for elements in clauses (see the syntax for more details). There are four such pronouns in Qevesa:

kar
Most commonly used, this pronoun references the focus of the first clause.
duol
Similar to kar, this pronoun references the focus of the previous clause.
kep
This pronoun references the entire following clause.
cëp
Similar to kep, this pronoun references the entire preceding clause.

They are all declined to the seven cases (but not to number):

  kar duol kep cëp
Dechticaetiative kara duola kepa cëpa
Ergative karan duolan kepan cëpan
Accusative karas duolas kepas cëpas
Partitive karav duolav kepav cëpav
Genetive karëki duolëki kepëki cëpëki
Essive karest duolest kepest cëpest
Translative karesk duolesk kepesk cëpesk
Postpositional karëð duolëð kepëð cëpëð

Interrogative Pronouns

There are three interrogative pronouns in Qevesa: loto, which is only used to refer to people; kenka, which is used to refer to other animate things, such as animals and some plants; and ëzac, which is used to refer to everything else.

  loto kenka ëzac
Dechticaetiative loto kenka ëzaca
Ergative loton kenkan ëzacan
Accusative lotos kenkas ëzacas
Partitive lotoiv kenkav ëzacav
Genetive lotoiki kenkaki ëzacëki
Essive – ëzacest
(when)
Translative –
Postpositional lotoið kenkað ëzacëð
(where, how)

Some examples:

  • Ëzaca turiottutt svo? "What is that?"
  • Loto turiottutt svo, tas karëð mity zeðoviňest qelutër? "Who was that I saw you with last night?"

Note that these interrogatives are never used as relative pronouns as is common in some other languages.
