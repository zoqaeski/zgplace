Qevesa Grammar

Conjunctions

Coordinating Conjunctions

The main coordinating conjunctions are used to join linguistic elements of equal importance. The following are the most important coordinating conjunctions in Qevesa. Many of them also act as delimiters.

le, ‘and’

Mazalátti inlásu zasými Aselió le Pelytzí nelesnosaš.

Aselio and Pelytzi are eating dinner now.

mëk, ‘and’
More often used to join clauses or phrases than single words; can also refer to addition.

Iknátu zalými Tešikzá ar örýnkirálešqi, mëk iknátu zalými jé ar atkérešqi.

Tešikza is going to the movies, and I am going to the theatre.

Lúvku zásúmi-mó ëtó mëk ëtótti nyštíš.

Two and two is not equal to five.

ës, ‘too’, ‘also’

Irnávu zisými ëva marosétti, tarosétti ës.

We have a bird and a fish (too).

món, ‘neither’, ‘nor’

Ňölka zisými-mó veza likáka jém, Zoqaëskím món.

I didn’t like that wine, and neither did Zoqaëski.
