Qevesa Grammar

The Noun Phrase

The noun phrase is comprised of several constituents, in the following order:

  1. Quantifier [optional]
  2. Demonstrative or Article [optional]
  3. Genetive/Possessive phrases [optional]
  4. Noun, declined to case and optionally number, except if noun is head of relative clause.
  5. Relative clauses [optional], chained with the proclitic el and terminated with relative clause case marker.

Use of the Cases

Qevesa has thirteen noun cases:

Nominative

The nominative marks the subject of the verb.

Ergative

The ergative marks the agent of the verb.

Accusative

The ergative marks the patient or recipient of the verb.

Dechticaetiative

The dechticaetiative marks the theme of the verb.

Genetive

The genetive marks more general or abstract relations between nouns than the possessive proclitic particle.

Essive

The essive marks duration or time and also indicates a temporary state of being.

Instrumental (-Comitative)

The instrumental (-comitative) marks the tool or manner by which the verb is done. It is also used to form adverbs.

Locative

The locative marks internal location.

Adessive

The adessive marks external location.

Allative

The allative marks motion towards the noun.

Illative

The illative marks motion from the exterior to the interior of the noun.

Ablative

The ablative marks motion away from noun.

Comparative

The comparative marks the standard by which something is compared.
