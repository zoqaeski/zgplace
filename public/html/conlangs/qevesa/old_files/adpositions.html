Qevesa Grammar

Adpositions

Qevesa features mostly postpositions; prepositions are usually only used before verb phrases. This section deals with both types of adposition and gives an explanation as to their usage. There are a number of types of adposition: locative, temporal, causal/resultative and relational. Note that the dictionary should be consulted to give a more concise lexicon.

Locative Adpositions

These adpositions describe a relationship in terms of physical location; and each has three forms, a static and two motive. The static locative adposition indicates a fixed location, and the motive adpositions indicate motion related to the adposition. The motive forms are indicated by an infix, -ȩt- for motion towards and -eck- for motion away.

Those nine main locative adpositions can be used as suffixes for a number or adjectives to create adpositional adjectives.

Temporal Adpositions

Temporal adpositions are similar to locative adpositions, differing only in that they describe a temporal relationship rather than a physical one. There are only a handful of these; the most common are listed below:

  • rypauvy after
  • kȩtoly since
  • zebeny before
  • vepady until
  • kopiršy during

Other Adpositions

There are a number of other adpositions:

  • ily by means of, instrument
  • mity1 in [a language], by use of
  • mity2 with, accompany
  • om(y) about, with regards to
