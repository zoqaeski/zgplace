Allophony

Most phonemes are pronounced as per the tables above, with few variations. However, the following allophones do exist:

  • [ç] is an allophone of /h/ that occurs before /i/
  • [s̪] is an allophone of /θ/ that occurs after /t/ in the affricate /ts̪/
  • [ɬ] is an allophone of /l/ that occurs after /t/
  • /ɾ/ is realised as [r] when word-initial or preconsonantal.
  • /ð/ may be realised as a fricative or an approximant.
  • /i u/ are realised as [j w] when syllable-initial.
  • /ʋ/ is often realised as [w] when intervocalic.
  • /j w/ are vocalised to [i u] when word-final or before consonants.
  • [j] drops out before /i iː/, and [w] drops out before /u uː/
  • [θ s̪] drops out before /s ð/
  • /h/ drops out before other consonants and when word-final
  • [æ] is an allophone of /a/ that occurs in words whose only other vowels consist of front vowels.
  • Affricates are often realised as their fricative counterparts when before another consonant.

Orthography

Qevesa is normally written using the kirna alphabet. However, for the purpose of this document a Latin transliteration is used, which matches one-to-one to the kirna script. Whilst it may have been more convenient to use a Latin orthography matched phonetically, the use of the system employed allows easier transcription into kirna, because Qevesa spelling is not entirely phonetic, with some phonemes having more than one orthographical representation. Attempts at an orthography reform have not met the success that the standard Qevesa dialect did; in hindsight it was probably an unwise idea to ceate a standard language without standardising the orthography at the same time.

The Latin transcription for Qevesa is shown below:

A A Á C D E É Ë F H I Í J K L M N Ň Ŋ O Ó Ö Ő P Q R S Š T U Ú V W Y Ý Z
a á a á c d e é ë f h i í j k l m n ň ŋ o ó ö ő p q r s š t u ú v w y ý z

For aesthetic reasons, characters in the orthography are bold rather than surrounded by angle brackets.

Punctuation

Formatting for full stops, commas, colons, exclamation/question marks, etc. is the same as in English with no space after the preceding word.

Single quotation marks are used to indicate emphasis on a word or phrase in writing, which in speech, would be indicated with a change in tone.

Guillemets (angle quotes, « ») are used to indicate speech in a similar manner to English, although with the following differences:

  • Commas are never used before or after the guillemets to mark interuptions (they serve this purpose as well);
  • Guillemets are always used to indicate speech, even paraphrased speech.

Quotations that span multiple paragraphs are block-quoted, with a left pointing guillemet at the start of the quotation and each new paragraph, and a right pointing guillemet at the end of the quotation.

Colons have an extra use not present in English: as an optional marker to separate acronyms and loan words from conjugations. The use with loan words is very rare, as most such words are usually naturalised by adopting to the standard Qevesa orthography.

Indentation is always used in written Qevesa to start a new paragraph, list entry, and heading, unless the text is centre-aligned.

Proclitics and enclitics are separated with hyphens unless the clitic has an elided vowel, in which case the clitic is separated from its head with an apostrophe.
